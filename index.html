Online services — no paper form

These were once paper forms but are now digital-only. Applicants sign in with a Government Gateway account and complete the process on GOV.UK.

C285

Repayment of Import Duty & VAT

Claim a repayment of overpaid import duty or VAT. CDS declarations must be claimed online — postal claims are no longer accepted. Three years for overpayments; one year for rejected imports (C&E1179).

Read the guidance →
CDS Claims

Claim via the Customs Declaration Service

The online route for repayment claims where the original declaration was made on CDS. Have all supporting documents ready to upload before starting.

Start a claim →
ToR1

Transfer of Residence Relief

Relief from customs charges on personal belongings when moving to the UK. HMRC issues a Unique Reference Number for the import declaration, used with procedure code 40 00 C01. Apply before goods are shipped.

Apply on GOV.UK →
C79

Import VAT Certificates

Monthly certificates showing import VAT paid — the evidence needed to reclaim import VAT on the VAT return. Retrieved digitally through the CDS financial dashboard.

Get your C79 →
i.

Declarations are electronic

The standard import declaration is made through the Customs Declaration Service, not on paper. Most traders appoint a customs agent or freight forwarder — the forms above support that process; they do not replace it.

ii.

VAT-registered? Use your return

VAT-registered businesses cannot reclaim overpaid import VAT through the C285 service. The correction is made as an adjustment on the VAT return instead — a frequent and costly point of confusion.

iii.

Postal imports are different

For parcels delivered by Royal Mail or Parcelforce, incorrect customs charges are challenged on form BOR286 — not C285. Check which carrier delivered the goods before choosing your route.
